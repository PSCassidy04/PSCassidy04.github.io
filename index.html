And that is where the proposed third division enters the field. As of last year the irish premier division became fully profsionalised with the last part-time club Drogheda United becoming fully profesional after its purchase by the trivella group and with full time clubs such as Cork City this season and the relegated Dundalk FC in the coming season fully professional clubs are begining to enter the first division of Irish football. this is a good thing to see as it will increase the competitiveness of clubs competing in the leagues. However it would also mean that the first division could become more lobsided when new clubs are added take for example Kerry FC who since their welcome introduction to the league in 2022 have finished dead last in 10th place for both season in a row. New Headway

Great news for irish football as representitives from the FAI general assembley voted to introduce the Aligned Football Calander for Ireland. What this would mean for irish football is that all the regional leagues such as the LSL (Leinster Senior League) and MSL (Munster Senior League) will change their league season and fixtures to run in conjunction with the league of ireland as Ireland is a summer league while other leagues in ireland run as standered, this will lead to better condition of pitches across ireland and with league timetables inline will hopefully lead to a proper footballing pyramid in ireland. This Meeting is the first step in a proposed 11 point pathways plan for irish football and is a great sign of change for footballing in ireland if you would like to learn more you can find the original article here if you want to continue to support and help grow football in ireland check out our page where you can find how you can support irish football!
